School Management Team

The School Management Team is responsible for the day-to-day operations and administration of Blessed Nursery & Primary School, ensuring the smooth running of all school activities and the implementation of policies set by the School Board. Our dedicated management team brings expertise, passion, and commitment to delivering excellence in education.

School Leadership

Head Teacher

Provides overall leadership and strategic direction for the school, ensuring academic excellence and institutional development.

Deputy Head Teacher

Assists the Head Teacher in managing school operations and oversees academic programs and curriculum implementation.

Management Team

Director of Studies

Coordinates academic activities, monitors teaching quality, and ensures curriculum standards are maintained.

School Administrator

Manages administrative functions, staff coordination, and ensures efficient school operations.

Bursar

Oversees financial management, budgeting, and resource allocation for the school.

Dean of Students

Responsible for student welfare, discipline, and pastoral care throughout the school.

Key Responsibilities

  • Implement policies and decisions of the School Board
  • Ensure quality education delivery and academic standards
  • Manage human resources and staff development
  • Oversee financial management and resource allocation
  • Maintain student welfare and discipline
  • Foster community relations and partnerships
  • Monitor and evaluate school performance
  • Coordinate curriculum development and implementation
  • Ensure compliance with educational regulations

Management Meetings

The School Management Team holds regular meetings to coordinate activities, address challenges, and ensure effective implementation of school policies and programs. The team works collaboratively to maintain high standards of education and operational excellence.

Blessed Nursery & Primary School is committed to providing quality education and fostering innovation for the betterment of society.
